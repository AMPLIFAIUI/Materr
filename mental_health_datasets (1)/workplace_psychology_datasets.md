
## Occupational Health Psychology (Johns Hopkins)

**Source:** Johns Hopkins Education and Research Center for Occupational Safety and Health. (n.d.). *ERC Occupational Health Psychology*. [Link](https://publichealth.jhu.edu/johns-hopkins-education-and-research-center-for-occupational-safety-and-health/erc-occupational-health-psychology)

**Summary:** This program at Johns Hopkins aims to educate doctoral-level research scientists in Occupational Health Psychology (OHP) and Total Worker Health (TWH). OHP applies psychology to improve worker well-being, safety, and health. The program addresses the need for graduate training in workforce mental health and aims to fill public health research gaps in OHP, healthy work design, and TWH. It includes coursework on psychological theories of work, workplace stressors, their effects, and health outcomes associated with poor mental health in the workplace. It also introduces holistic approaches to benefit worker physical and mental health by addressing psychosocial factors, organizational conditions, and environmental exposures.

**Key Findings/Focus Areas:**
*   Focus on the application of psychology to improve worker well-being, safety, and health.
*   Addresses mental health in the workplace, including stressors and their impact.
*   Emphasizes a holistic approach to worker health, considering psychosocial factors and organizational conditions.
*   Aims to train PhD-level researchers in this field.
